The names of user-defined classes and functions, as well as built-in constructs and keywords (such as echo, while, class, etc.), are caseinsensitive.
Variables, on the other hand, are case-sensitive. That is, $name, $NAME, and $NaME are three different variables.
curly braces to mark a block of code, such as a conditional test or loop, does not need a semicolon after a closing brace. Unlike in other languages, in PHP the semicolon before the closing brace is not optional, it's necessary.
The semicolon, however, is optional before a closing PHP tag - "?>"
